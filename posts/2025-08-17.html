List of Social Norms

What does it mean to be "normal"? What behaviors are expected / not expected in a given situation? I have struggled with this question my whole life, sometimes in attempts to fit in, and sometimes not to. Most of us would probably say that there is no such thing as normal. However, based on my experience and observations, there is defitely a set of behaviors and social rules which are generally expected, and I will call this "normal" behavior or social norms. THe purpose of this possibly incomplete, possible redundant list is mostly descriptive; I am not trying to assign value judgements just yet. Additionally, this mostly refelcts my experience as a young adult in American society, and thus mostly focuses on American norms.

Material

  • Clothing and fashion
    • Makeup, shaving, jewelry, etc.
  • Wedding ring
    • The groom is typically expected to buy a wedding ring for his spouse
  • Dowry (south asia) ** Bride expected to pay / give something of large value to the groom's family upon marriage
  • Man expected to be the primary earner, woman expected to take care of house
  • Drinking - you are expected to drink with others as a social comradery
  • Giving gifts and presents

Conversational

  • "Do you want to do X" = Command/request do do X
  • Correct amount of time to use in a converstaions
  • Small Talk
  • No politics / divisive / potentially offensive subjects
  • What is defined as "funny"
  • Saying platitudes: just be yourself, it'll work out eventually, everything happens for a reason, everything works out eventually, etc
  • Maintaining eye contact
  • Handshaking, hugging, physical greeting
  • Body Language -- this is it's own entire can of worms
  • Verbally acknowledging sneezes
  • Flirting / Innuendos / Double Meaning
  • Criticism/disagrement is taboo
  • Awkward Silence is taboo - especially among people who don't know each other as well
  • Tipping Culture
    • Not expressing certain emotions - For men, probably sadness/vulnerability -- for women, probably aggressiveness/anger
  • Rules for messaging online -- I personally don't know any rules off the top of my head, but not sure
  • Knowing how to dance

Societal

  • Gender norms
  • Respecting elders and seniors, authority, etc.
  • Class heiarachies
  • Some traditions
    • Holidays
  • Peronality Archetypes ** (Nerd, Jock, "Loser", alpha male, beta male, etc.)
  • Honor Culture - Saving face, not making a fool of yourself in front of other people, etc.
  • Halo effect / Hero worship People who are more attractive, more wealthy, more famous, automatically given special privileges
  • In-group / Outgroup - must have similar interests/likes/goals to other people in your same "group"

Other

  • telling the truth versus being nice -- often the latter is preferred
  • Being humble (sometimes)
  • Elbows on table
  • Double Standards

I've probably missed a ton of other common ones, please tell me if you think of any
