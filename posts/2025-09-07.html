Taking Notes

It's often a given that we should take notes. But is this true? How should I take notes, if at all? I think the answer depends on a number of factors, such as the kind of class I am taking and also the resources the teacher provides. Conventional wisdom says pick the strategy that fits your learning style, but I want to go more into detail on this and focus on stuff that is less nebulous.

For my purposes I will focus on the issue of taking notes in STEM / math-heavy / practice-oriented classes, as those are more relevant to my situation. I will focus on the extremes (taking comprehensive notes and not taking any notes), and then come up with a comprimise between the two that I want to try

Reasons to take comprehensive notes

This is the strategy I have been following for most of my life.

Taking notes:

  • Better for remembering stuff without the context of a problem
  • Better for recalling stuff after class is done
  • Can be neater and more concise than the material provided
  • Better for putting things in a format that is specific to your understanding of the material, not someone else's

Disadvantages:

  • Much more time - this is the biggest disadvantage, means there is less time to do practice problems and read the material before lecture
  • Harder on the wrist

Reasons to not take comprehensive notes

Don't take notes (rely on lecture notes):

  • MUCH less time required to get through the reading material
  • More time to do practice problems
  • Might be able concentrate on understanding lecture better

Disadvantages:

  • Hard to recall little details that the professor says but doesn't write down
  • Less convenient to recall information long-term

Compromise: Take Summary Notes, not comprehensive

  • Notes should be designed as a quick reference for solving problems
    • No derivations, those should be performed as practice problems or referenced as-needed from the textbook
    • For important examples, write down the setup of the example/key results, but not the derivation
  • No latex: learning curve is too high, takes too much time otherwise
  • If something has detailed notes already available, only take summary / cheat sheets notes, not detailed notes
  • If something has a textbook, take summary notes BEFORE lecture
